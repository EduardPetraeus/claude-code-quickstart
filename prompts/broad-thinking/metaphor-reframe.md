# Metaphor Reframe

## Purpose
Break out of technical thinking patterns by mapping the problem onto other domains.

## Prompt Template

```
Describe this problem as if it were:

1. **An ecosystem in nature** — What are the organisms? What's the food chain? What's the environment? Where is the symbiosis, and where is the parasitism?

2. **A city's infrastructure** — What are the roads, buildings, utilities? Where are the bottlenecks? What happens during rush hour? Where would a city planner invest?

3. **A game with players and rules** — Who are the players? What are they optimizing for? What are the rules they follow (and which ones they break)? What would a game designer change?

4. **A human body** — What's the skeleton (structure)? What's the nervous system (communication)? What's the immune system (protection)? What organ is failing?

For each metaphor:
- Map the key elements of the problem onto the metaphor
- What insight does this metaphor reveal that technical framing hides?
- What solution does the metaphor suggest?

Finally: which metaphor was most illuminating, and what concrete action does it suggest?
```

## When to Use
- Architecture decisions — metaphors reveal structural patterns
- Debugging complex systems — find the "failing organ"
- When technical analysis keeps leading to the same conclusions
